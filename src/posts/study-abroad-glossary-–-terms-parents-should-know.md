As a parent in Nepal supporting your child's dream to study abroad, you'll encounter many new terms and acronyms. Understanding this jargon can help you feel more confident and informed throughout the process. This glossary explains some common study abroad terms.

For more parent-specific advice, see our guides on [Sponsoring Your Child's Study Abroad](/blog/sponsoring-a-child-for-study-abroad-–-what-parents-need) and [What Parents Should Know Before Sending Their Child Abroad](/blog/what-parents-should-know-before-sending-their-child-abroad).

## Key Study Abroad Terms Explained

*   **Academic Transcripts:** Official records of your child's grades and subjects from their previous educational institutions (e.g., SEE/SLC, +2, Bachelor's).

*   **Offer Letter (Conditional/Unconditional):**
    *   **Conditional Offer:** An offer of admission from a university that requires your child to meet certain conditions (e.g., achieve specific grades, submit English test scores) before it becomes firm.
    *   **Unconditional Offer:** An offer with no pending academic or English conditions. Your child has met all requirements for admission.
    *   Learn more: [How to Read and Understand an Offer Letter](/blog/how-to-read-and-understand-a-coe-or-offer-letter).

*   **CoE (Confirmation of Enrolment) - Australia:** An official document issued by an Australian education provider once tuition fees are paid. It's required to apply for an Australian student visa.

*   **I-20 (Certificate of Eligibility for Nonimmigrant Student Status) - USA:** A document issued by a U.S. SEVP-certified school. Required to apply for an F-1 student visa and to enter the U.S.

*   **CAS (Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies) - UK:** A unique reference number provided by a UK sponsoring institution (university). It's an electronic record needed for a UK Student Visa application.

*   **DLI (Designated Learning Institution) - Canada:** A school approved by a provincial or territorial government to host international students. Your child must have an acceptance letter from a DLI to apply for a Canadian study permit.

*   **GTE (Genuine Temporary Entrant) - Australia:** A requirement for the Australian student visa. Your child must demonstrate a genuine intention to stay in Australia temporarily for study purposes and to return to Nepal after their studies. A strong GTE statement is crucial.
    *   Learn more: [What is the GTE Requirement?](/blog/what-is-the-genuine-temporary-entrant-(gte)-requirement)
    *   And: [How to Write a Strong GTE Statement](/blog/how-to-write-a-strong-gte-statement-for-australia)

*   **SOP (Statement of Purpose):** An essay written by your child explaining their academic background, reasons for choosing a specific course and university, career goals, and why they are a suitable candidate. It's a key part of university applications.
    *   Learn more: [How to Write an SOP That Stands Out](/blog/how-to-write-a-statement-of-purpose-that-stands-out).

*   **LOR (Letter of Recommendation):** A letter written by a teacher, professor, or employer recommending your child for admission.

*   **IELTS (International English Language Testing System):** A widely recognized English proficiency test.
    *   Learn more: [IELTS vs PTE vs TOEFL](/blog/ielts-vs-pte-vs-toefl_-which-one-should-you-take)
    *   And: [How Long to Prepare for IELTS?](/blog/how-long-does-it-take-to-prepare-for-ielts)

*   **PTE Academic (Pearson Test of English Academic):** Another popular computer-based English proficiency test.

*   **TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language):** An English test primarily used for admission to U.S. universities.

*   **SEVIS (Student and Exchange Visitor Information System) - USA:** A U.S. government database that tracks international students. The I-20 form contains a SEVIS ID, and students must pay a SEVIS I-901 fee.

*   **OSHC (Overseas Student Health Cover) - Australia:** Mandatory health insurance for international students studying in Australia.
    *   Learn more: [What is OSHC and Why Does it Matter?](/blog/what-is-oshc-why-overseas-health-cover-matters)

*   **GIC (Guaranteed Investment Certificate) - Canada:** For the Student Direct Stream (SDS) visa route to Canada, applicants must show proof of a GIC of CAD $10,000 (amount subject to change by IRCC).
    *   Learn more: [Canada SDS vs Non-SDS Visa Route](/blog/canada-sds-vs-non-sds-visa-route-–-which-is-better)

*   **Intake:** The period when a university begins its academic session (e.g., Fall Intake, Spring Intake, February Intake, July Intake). [When to Start Your Application for July 2026 Intake](/blog/when-should-i-start-my-application-for-july-2026-intake).

*   **Credit Hours/Units:** The number of hours or units assigned to a course, indicating its workload or academic value.

*   **GPA (Grade Point Average):** A numerical representation of your child's academic performance.

*   **Pathway Program/Foundation Course:** A preparatory course designed to help international students meet the entry requirements for a Bachelor's degree or to bridge academic gaps.

*   **Post-Study Work Visa (e.g., PGWP in Canada, Graduate Route in UK, Subclass 485 in Australia):** A visa that allows eligible international students to stay and work in the country for a certain period after graduation. This can be a pathway to [Permanent Residency (PR)](/blog/which-country-gives-pr-faster-after-study).

*   **Accreditation:** Official recognition that an institution or program meets required quality standards. It's important to choose accredited institutions. (See [PixarEdu's Accreditations](/about) - *ensure anchor exists*).
