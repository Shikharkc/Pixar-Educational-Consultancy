## Why Use a Scholarship Tracker?

*   **Organization:** Keep track of multiple scholarships, their deadlines, and requirements.
*   **Avoid Missing Deadlines:** A clear overview of application timelines.
*   **Track Progress:** Monitor the status of each application.
*   **Store Information:** Centralize links, contact details, and notes for each scholarship.
*   **Efficiency:** Save time by having all data readily accessible.

## What to Include in Your Scholarship Tracker (Google Sheet Columns)

Here are suggested columns for your Google Sheet template:

1.  **Scholarship Name:** (e.g., "University X International Excellence Scholarship")
2.  **University/Organization:** (Name of the institution or body offering it)
3.  **Country:** (Country where the university/organization is based - e.g., [USA](/country-guides#usa), [Australia](/country-guides#australia))
4.  **Eligibility Criteria (Brief):** (e.g., "Nepalese citizen, Bachelor's in Engineering, IELTS 7.0")
5.  **Scholarship Amount/Value:** (e.g., "AUD 10,000 per year", "Full Tuition Waiver", "25% of tuition")
6.  **Application Deadline:** (Crucial! Use a consistent date format, e.g., YYYY-MM-DD)
7.  **Link to Scholarship Page:** (Direct URL for easy access)
8.  **Required Documents:** (e.g., "SOP, LORs, Transcript, Essay on Topic X")
    *   Consider using checkboxes or a separate notes section for this.
    *   Our [Document Checklist tool](/ai-assistants#document-checklist) can help identify general application documents.
9.  **Application Status:** (e.g., "To Apply", "Researching", "Drafting Essay", "Applied", "Awaiting Result", "Successful", "Unsuccessful") - *Use a dropdown list for this.*
10. **Date Applied:** (Date you submitted the application)
11. **Result Date (Expected/Actual):**
12. **Notes:** (Any specific points, contact person, essay topics, reminders)
13. **Requires Separate Application?** (Yes/No - some scholarships are automatic upon admission, others need a distinct application)

## How to Create and Use Your Google Sheet Tracker

1.  **Create a New Google Sheet:** Go to [sheets.google.com](https://sheets.google.com) and start a new spreadsheet.
2.  **Set Up Columns:** Add the column headers suggested above, or customize them to your needs.
3.  **Format:**
    *   Use **Data Validation** for columns like "Application Status" to create dropdown menus.
    *   Use **Conditional Formatting** to highlight approaching deadlines (e.g., turn cells red if the deadline is within 7 days).
    *   **Freeze** the header row so it's always visible when scrolling.
4.  **Start Populating:** As you research scholarships, enter the information into your tracker immediately.
5.  **Update Regularly:** Keep the status and notes for each scholarship current.
6.  **Sort and Filter:** Use Google Sheets' sorting and filtering capabilities to view scholarships by deadline, country, or status.
